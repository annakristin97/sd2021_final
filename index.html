Under what circumstances are accidents more likely to occur?

Traffic collisions are one of the most pressing problems facing the world, as they result in numerous deaths, injuries, and fatalities each year, as well as significant economic losses. With the help of data analysis, the question “Under what circumstances are traffic accidents more likely to occur?” will be explored and answered.
To do so, historic data from the city of Seattle is used from the years 2004 to the end of 2020. Different data visualizations are used to present the variant results that were explored and to highlight key findings.
This analysis is designated for the healthcare system, furthermore the four main hospitals that have ER’s (Emergency Room) in Seattle. The analysis is done to explore how different circumstances and different time periods can influence the severity of an accident and how the hospitals can be better prepared when those severity levels occur.

Heatmaps

Looking at heatmaps for different times of the year when a collision was logged, the heatmap displayes the count of accurances at the different time period. The times looked at include logs happening every month, weeks, day and hour of day.

Looking at the three different heatmaps, it can be concluded that there are obvious trends on traffic collision times.
On the first graph it can be seen that accidents are distributed down to each weekday happening over the month. There isn't that much of a variance between the months, but more variance between the weekdays, for example sundays are not many collision logs. October seems to be the busiest month and is therefore colored little darker than other months.
Second graph shows accidents every weekday distributed over the weeks of the year. Again there is little variance between weeks, but obvious difference between weekdays.
The third graphs shows accidents happening each hour of the weekday. It can be seen that accidents are most common during working weekdays and on the time people tend drive home from work, that is between 15:00 - 18:00 o'clock.

So what do these result mean for hospitals?

Everyday there are many accidents happening, but only fraction of the all accident need to go to the hospital and that is only from sever accidents. The previous results gave a good indication the busiest time of traffic collisions, but what other factors are the also one of the reasons for sever accidents that need a hospital visit.

Normalised Histogram Graphs

A normalised count of collision logs are shown on five different categories:

  • The collisions Type
  • Hour of the Day
  • Road Condiditon
  • Light Condidtion
  • Driving Under Influence (DUI)
    • Each category is shown in different tab with the four severity levels, they are; 1, 2, 2b and 3, where 1 is he least sever type of collision and 3 is the most sever type of collsion. The severity levels are shown in diverse colors based on their severity factor.

    Based on the results, it is obvious that the type of collision, road and light condidtion and if driver was under influence, play a huge role in the severity level. It was decided to look deeper into the severity levels 2, 2b and 3 since they are the collision types that need to go to the hospital.

    Interactive map

    Which of the accidents are sever and hospitalisation is needed? In the interactive map, collisions logs are colored by their severity, where severity code 2 is colored orange, 2b is dark orange and 3 is colored red. Markers has been placed on the map where the next Emergency Room (ER) is located. The interactive map, allows the features to select the type of severity to be displayed on the map itself.
